## T1-Weighted (Structural) Image Processing

### 1. Intensity Non-Uniformity Correction

**Purpose:**  
MRI scanners often produce images with smooth intensity variations across the field-of-view, known as "bias fields" or "shading artifacts."

**Method:**  
Uses ANTs' `N4BiasFieldCorrection` algorithm to estimate and remove low-frequency intensity variations.

**Why It Matters:**  
- Ensures tissue intensities are consistent across the brain
- Critical for accurate tissue segmentation and registration
- Improves the quality of all downstream processing steps

---

### 2. Skull Stripping

**Purpose:**  
Removes non-brain tissue (scalp, skull, eyes, neck) from the image.

**Method:**  
Uses ANTs' `antsBrainExtraction.sh`, an atlas-based method that warps a template brain mask to the subject's anatomy.

**Quality Control:**  
fMRIPrep generates visual reports showing the brain mask overlaid on the image for verification.

**Why It Matters:**  
- Prevents non-brain tissue from interfering with registration and analysis
- Reduces computational load for subsequent steps
- Improves accuracy of tissue segmentation

---

### 3. Tissue Segmentation

**Purpose:**  
Classifies each voxel as gray matter (GM), white matter (WM), or cerebrospinal fluid (CSF).

**Method:**  
Uses FSL's `FAST` (FMRIB's Automated Segmentation Tool), which employs:
- Hidden Markov random field model
- Expectation-maximization algorithm
- Intensity-based classification

**Outputs:**  
- Probability maps for each tissue class (GM, WM, CSF)
- Used for confound extraction
- Used for boundary-based registration

**Why It Matters:**  
- Enables extraction of physiological noise confounds
- Critical for accurate BBR registration
- Supports tissue-specific analyses

---

### 4. Non-linear Normalization to MNI152NLin2009cAsym

**Purpose:**  
Warps the subject's brain anatomy into a standardized coordinate space.

**Template:**  
**MNI152NLin2009cAsym** - A modern, asymmetric template that better preserves anatomical detail than older symmetric versions.

**Method:**  
Uses ANTs' `antsRegistration` with:
- Multi-stage registration
- Mutual information-based optimization
- Non-linear warping to account for individual anatomical differences

**Why It Matters:**  
- Enables group-level analyses by aligning all subjects to the same anatomical framework
- Facilitates comparison across studies
- Allows use of standard atlases and coordinates

## BOLD (Functional) Time Series Processing

### 1. Generation of a BOLD Reference Volume

**Purpose:**  
Creates a single high-quality representative image from the 4D fMRI time series.

**Method:**  
- Detects and excludes non-steady-state volumes ("dummy scans")
- Computes a median or mean of motion-corrected volumes
- Maximizes contrast and minimizes noise

**Why It Matters:**  
- Provides a stable reference for motion correction
- Used for registration to structural images
- Improves accuracy of all subsequent alignment steps

---

### 2. Head Motion Correction (Realignment)

**Purpose:**  
Compensates for small head movements during scanning that can create artifactual signal changes.

**Method:**  
Uses FSL's `mcflirt` to estimate:
- 6 rigid-body parameters per volume:
  - 3 translations (x, y, z)
  - 3 rotations (pitch, roll, yaw)
- Resamples all volumes to align them to the reference

**Outputs:**  
- Motion parameters saved as confounds for later regression
- Motion-corrected 4D time series

**Important Notes:**  
- Motion is estimated *before* slice-timing correction for more accurate parameter estimation
- Framewise Displacement (FD) and DVARS are calculated for quality control

**Why It Matters:**  
- Head motion is a major source of artifact in fMRI
- Uncorrected motion can lead to false positives/negatives
- Motion parameters can be regressed out during statistical analysis

---

### 3. Co-registration: BOLD to T1w via Boundary-Based Registration (BBR)

**Purpose:**  
Aligns the functional (EPI) images to the high-resolution structural (T1w) image.

**Method:**  
Uses FreeSurfer's `bbregister` (or FSL's `flirt` with BBR cost function if FreeSurfer is disabled).

**How BBR Works:**  
- Optimizes alignment by matching the EPI image's intensity gradient
- Specifically targets the gray/white matter boundary identified in T1w segmentation
- More robust than intensity-based methods for EPI-to-structural registration

**Why BBR?**  
- EPI images have distortions and low contrast
- Intensity-based registration often fails with EPI data
- BBR leverages anatomical information from the structural scan

**Why It Matters:**  
- Accurate functional-to-structural alignment is critical for:
  - Anatomical localization of activations
  - Combining functional and structural information
  - Subsequent normalization to standard space

---

### 4. Transformation into MNI Space Using Composed Transformations

**Purpose:**  
Maps the preprocessed BOLD data from native functional space → T1w space → MNI standard space.

**Method:**  
1. **Concatenates all transforms** into a single composite warp:
   - BOLD to T1w transform
   - T1w to MNI transform
   
2. **Applies the composite transform in ONE interpolation step** using a high-quality Lanczos kernel

**Key Advantage:**  
- **Minimizes interpolation-induced smoothing** and signal loss
- Preserves spatial resolution and statistical power
- Avoids multiple resampling steps that degrade data quality

**Traditional Approach (Multiple Interpolations):**  
```
BOLD native → BOLD corrected → BOLD to T1w → T1w to MNI
     ↓              ↓                ↓              ↓
  Interp 1       Interp 2         Interp 3       Interp 4
```

**fMRIPrep Approach (Single Interpolation):**  
```
BOLD native → MNI space
     ↓
  Single interpolation with composite transform
```

**Why It Matters:**  
- Each interpolation introduces smoothing and potential artifacts
- Preserving spatial resolution increases statistical power
- Critical for high-resolution fMRI and small structure analyses

## Key Design Principles of fMRIPrep

### 1. Minimal Preprocessing
- **Intentionally avoids** steps that limit downstream analysis choices
- No spatial smoothing
- No temporal filtering
- No global signal regression
- Allows researchers to make these decisions at the analysis stage

### 2. Transparent Reporting
- Generates detailed **HTML quality-control reports** for every subject
- Shows key preprocessing checkpoints with visual overlays
- Includes metrics like:
  - Motion parameters
  - Framewise Displacement
  - Registration quality
  - Brain extraction accuracy

### 3. BIDS-Compliant
- Requires input data in **Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS)** format
- Enables automatic metadata extraction
- Promotes reproducible workflows
- Standardizes data organization

### 4. One-Shot Interpolation
- Concatenates all spatial transforms
- Applies them in a single interpolation step
- Reduces interpolation artifacts
- Preserves data quality

### 5. Flexible Outputs
- Can output data in multiple spaces:
  - Native functional space
  - T1w space
  - MNI standard space
  - fsaverage surface space
- Multiple formats:
  - NIfTI (volumetric)
  - CIFTI (surface + volumetric)
  - GIFTI (surface)
- Supports diverse analysis approaches

## Output Files

### Preprocessed Data
- `*_space-MNI152NLin2009cAsym_desc-preproc_bold.nii.gz` - Preprocessed BOLD in MNI space
- `*_space-T1w_desc-preproc_bold.nii.gz` - Preprocessed BOLD in T1w space
- `*_desc-preproc_T1w.nii.gz` - Preprocessed T1w image

### Confounds
- `*_desc-confounds_regressors.tsv` - Confound variables including:
  - Motion parameters (6 rigid-body)
  - Framewise Displacement (FD)
  - DVARS
  - aCompCor components
  - tCompCor components
  - Outlier fractions

### Masks and Segmentations
- `*_desc-brain_mask.nii.gz` - Brain mask
- `*_desc-aseg_dseg.nii.gz` - Anatomical segmentation
- `*_desc-aparcaseg_dseg.nii.gz` - FreeSurfer parcellation

### Transforms
- `*_from-bold_to-T1w_mode-image_xfm.txt` - BOLD to T1w transform
- `*_from-T1w_to-MNI152NLin2009cAsym_mode-image_xfm.h5` - T1w to MNI transform

### Quality Control
- `sub-*_.html` - Visual QC report
- `figures/` - QC images and metrics

### Metadata
- `dataset_description.json` - Dataset information
- `participants.tsv` - Participant information
- `scans.tsv` - Scan timing information

## Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **BOLD** | Blood Oxygen Level Dependent; the contrast mechanism used in functional MRI |
| **EPI** | Echo Planar Imaging; fast MRI sequence used for fMRI |
| **BBR** | Boundary-Based Registration; method for aligning EPI to structural images |
| **MNI** | Montreal Neurological Institute; standard brain template space |
| **CompCor** | Component-based noise correction; method for extracting physiological noise |
| **FD** | Framewise Displacement; measure of head motion between volumes |
| **DVARS** | Temporal derivative of variance; measure of signal change between volumes |
| **NIfTI** | Neuroimaging Informatics Technology Initiative; standard file format |
| **BIDS** | Brain Imaging Data Structure; standardized organization format |
